ABOUT

THE FASCINATION

I'm fascinated by how simple molecular rules give rise to the staggering complexity of living systems. My work sits at the intersection of biology, physics, and computation—using mathematical frameworks to uncover principles that govern cellular behavior.

WHAT MAKES MY APPROACH DIFFERENT

I've spent years in the lab—culturing cells, optimizing biochemical assays, watching biological systems respond to perturbations in real-time. This hands-on experience gives me an intuition for which theoretical questions actually matter and which models will break when confronted with biological messiness.

Now I'm focused on the theory: building computational models that don't just fit data, but reveal mechanism. Models that generate testable predictions. Models that connect molecular-scale interactions to cellular-scale phenotypes.

MY PATH

🔬 EXPERIMENTAL FOUNDATION 2015-2021

Started in biophysics labs at McGill and Drexel, studying DNA dynamics and drug delivery systems. Learned that biology is fundamentally a physical system—governed by thermodynamics, kinetics, and statistical mechanics.

🧬 BIOENGINEERING REALITY CHECK 2020-2021

Led biomanufacturing for cellular therapeutics at Stemcellerant. Nothing teaches you about cellular heterogeneity, stochastic processes, and system robustness like trying to manufacture consistent cell products at scale.

💡 COMPUTATIONAL PIVOT 2021-Present

Realized the most impactful questions I could ask required moving beyond what's measurable to what's computable. Now combining AI/ML with mechanistic modeling to tackle problems in cellular decision-making and gene regulation.

📊 CURRENT FOCUS 2024-Present

Exploring how AI can accelerate biological discovery—not as a black box, but as a tool for theory building. Working at the intersection of machine learning and biophysical modeling.
